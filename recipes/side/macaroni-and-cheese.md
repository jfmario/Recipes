# Macaroni and Cheese

This baked macaroni and cheese is scaled as a side dish for 8 servings, with a creamy cheddar sauce and toasted breadcrumb topping.

Shred the cheese before starting the sauce so it melts smoothly and the stovetop steps stay controlled.

## Ingredients

* 3 cups dry elbow macaroni
* 5 cups shredded sharp cheddar cheese
* 4 tbsp butter
* 1 garlic clove
* 4 tbsp all-purpose flour
* 4 cups whole milk
* 1 cup sour cream
* 1 cup plain breadcrumbs

## Steps

* Bring a large pot of water to a boil over high heat while you prep the rest of the ingredients.
* Mince 1 garlic clove, shred 5 cups cheddar cheese if needed, and measure 4 tbsp butter, 4 tbsp flour, 4 cups milk, 1 cup sour cream, and 1 cup breadcrumbs.
* Preheat the oven to 450F and set out a 9x13-inch baking dish.
* Add 3 cups dry elbow macaroni to the boiling water with a pinch of salt and cook to al dente according to package timing, usually 7 to 9 minutes. Drain well and set aside.
* Melt 4 tbsp butter in a large heavy pot or Dutch oven over medium-low heat.
* Add the minced 1 garlic clove and cook for 1 minute, stirring constantly so it softens without browning.
* Sprinkle in 4 tbsp flour and whisk for 1 minute to form a smooth paste.
* Slowly pour in 4 cups milk while whisking continuously, then raise heat to medium and cook until the sauce reaches a gentle boil and thickens enough to coat a spoon, 4 to 6 minutes.
* Turn off the heat, then whisk in 5 cups shredded cheddar cheese in 3 additions until fully melted and smooth.
* Whisk in 1 cup sour cream, then season with 2 tsp kosher salt and 1/2 tsp black pepper.
* Fold the drained macaroni into the cheese sauce and stir until all 3 cups cooked macaroni are evenly coated.
* Transfer the macaroni and cheese to the baking dish, spread evenly, and sprinkle 1 cup breadcrumbs over the top.
* Bake at 450F for 12 to 15 minutes, until the top is golden and the edges are bubbling.
* Rest for 5 minutes before serving.
