# Cilantro Lime Rice

This version is written for 8 side servings (about 3/4 cup cooked rice each). Toasting the dry rice in oil before adding liquid helps keep the grains separate and fluffy. Use vegetable broth if you want the dish vegan, vegetarian, meat-free, or suitable for Lent; chicken broth adds a different depth.

Do not rinse the rice first; the toasting step replaces that habit from plain absorption rice.

## Ingredients

* 2 cups long-grain white rice (jasmine or basmati)
* 4 cups low-sodium vegetable broth or chicken broth
* 1 cup roughly chopped fresh cilantro leaves

## Steps

* Roughly chop fresh cilantro until you have 1 cup measured, lightly packed, and set it aside. Juice 2 limes into a small bowl and set the juice aside.
* Heat 2 tbsp plus 2 tsp vegetable oil in a 12-inch skillet with a tight-fitting lid over medium heat until the oil shimmers.
* Add 2 cups long-grain white rice to the skillet. If you want salt in the pot, sprinkle in about 2/3 tsp kosher salt. Cook, stirring often, until the grains look lightly toasted and smell nutty, about 4 to 5 minutes.
* Pour in 4 cups vegetable broth or chicken broth, raise the heat, and bring to a full boil. Stir once to settle the rice, then reduce the heat to the lowest setting that maintains a bare simmer, cover the skillet, and cook for 20 minutes or until the liquid is fully absorbed.
* Remove the skillet from the heat. Fluff the rice with a fork or spoon, then immediately stir in the reserved lime juice and 1 cup chopped cilantro until evenly distributed.
* Taste and add a pinch more salt if needed, then serve warm. If the rice sits before serving, keep the lid on so it does not dry out; for make-ahead batches, cool and refrigerate, then reheat with a splash of broth or water, covering the pan so it steams, and add fresh lime juice and cilantro after reheating if you want the brightest finish.
