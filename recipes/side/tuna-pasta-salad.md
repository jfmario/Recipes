# Tuna Pasta Salad

This is a cold pasta salad scaled for eight side portions (about 1 cup each).

Chop the vegetables while the pasta water comes to a boil so everything is ready when the pasta finishes.

## Ingredients

* 4 cups dry rotini or fusilli pasta
* 1 cup frozen peas
* 1 cup carrots, chopped
* 1 cup celery, chopped
* 1 cup grape tomatoes, halved or quartered
* 3 (4 oz) cans tuna packed in water, drained well
* 1/3 cup plain Greek yogurt
* 1/3 cup mayonnaise
* 1 tbsp red wine vinegar
* 1 tbsp Dijon mustard

## Steps

* Fill a large pot with water, salt it generously, and bring it to a boil over high heat.
* While the water heats, chop 1 cup carrots and 1 cup celery into small bite-sized pieces and slice 1 cup grape tomatoes.
* Add 4 cups dry pasta and cook according to the package directions for al dente. When about 4 minutes of cook time remain, add 1 cup frozen peas to the same pot so they cook with the pasta.
* Drain the pasta and peas in a colander, rinse under cold water until cool, shake off excess water, and set aside.
* Open and drain 3 (4 oz) cans tuna, pressing out excess liquid with the back of a fork.
* In a large mixing bowl, combine the chopped 1 cup carrots, 1 cup celery, 1 cup grape tomatoes, and drained tuna.
* In a small bowl, whisk 1/3 cup Greek yogurt, 1/3 cup mayonnaise, 1 tbsp red wine vinegar, 1 tbsp Dijon mustard, 2 minced garlic cloves, 1 tsp dried dill, 1 tsp kosher salt, and 1/2 tsp black pepper until smooth.
* Add the cooled pasta and peas to the large bowl, pour the dressing over everything, and fold until evenly coated.
* Taste and add a pinch more salt or pepper if needed, then serve chilled or at cool room temperature. Refrigerate leftovers in a sealed container for up to 5 days.
