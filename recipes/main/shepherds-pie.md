# Shepherd's Pie

This shepherd's pie is written for 8 servings and uses your existing mashed potatoes recipe as the topping so the workflow stays consistent.

Use 1 full batch of `Mashed Potatoes` for the potato layer.

## Ingredients

* 2 lb ground lamb or ground beef
* 1 medium onion
* 4 garlic cloves
* 1 (16 oz) bag frozen mixed vegetables
* 1 1/2 cups beef broth
* 3 tbsp tomato paste
* 2 tbsp Worcestershire sauce
* 2 tbsp arrowroot flour
* 1 full batch prepared mashed potatoes from the `Mashed Potatoes` recipe.

## Steps

* Start 1 full batch of mashed potatoes from  and time it so the potatoes are ready to spread during assembly.
* Dice 1 medium onion and mince 4 garlic cloves. Measure 3 tsp dried parsley, 1 1/2 tsp dried rosemary, 1 1/2 tsp dried thyme, 2 tsp kosher salt, and 1 tsp black pepper into a small bowl.
* Preheat the oven to 400 F and place a 9x13-inch baking dish nearby.
* Heat 1 tbsp neutral oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat for about 1 minute. Add the diced onion and cook for 5 minutes, stirring occasionally, until softened.
* Add 2 lb ground lamb or ground beef to the skillet and break it up with a spatula. Cook for 7 minutes until mostly browned.
* Add the minced 4 garlic cloves, the prepared herb bowl, and 2 tbsp Worcestershire sauce. Stir for 30 seconds until fragrant.
* Sprinkle 2 tbsp arrowroot flour over the meat mixture and stir for 1 minute so no dry pockets remain.
* Stir in 3 tbsp tomato paste, then add 1 1/2 cups beef broth and 1 (16 oz) bag frozen mixed vegetables. Bring the mixture to a boil over medium-high heat.
* Reduce heat to medium-low and simmer for 5 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the filling thickens slightly.
* Transfer the hot meat filling to the 9x13-inch baking dish and spread it into an even layer.
* Spoon 1 full batch of prepared mashed potatoes over the filling and spread to the edges in an even layer, sealing the perimeter.
* Bake uncovered at 400 F for 25 minutes until bubbling around the edges and lightly golden on top.
* Rest the shepherd's pie for 15 minutes before serving so the layers set cleanly.
