# Swedish Meatballs

These Swedish meatballs are scaled for 8 servings, yielding about 32 medium meatballs with enough sauce to coat and serve generously.

Use full-fat sour cream and temper it with warm sauce before adding it to the pan so the gravy stays smooth and does not break.

## Ingredients

* 3/4 cup yellow onion, finely diced
* 3 garlic cloves, minced
* 3/4 cup panko breadcrumbs
* 3/8 cup Parmesan cheese, finely grated
* 1 1/2 lb ground beef
* 1 lb ground pork
* 6 tbsp olive oil, divided
* 6 tbsp butter
* 6 tbsp all-purpose flour
* 3 cups beef broth
* 1 1/2 cubes chicken bouillon
* 3 tsp Worcestershire sauce
* 1 1/2 tsp Dijon mustard
* 3/4 cup sour cream, room temperature
* Fresh parsley, chopped, for garnish (optional)
* Mashed potatoes or egg noodles, for serving

## Steps

* Dice 3/4 cup yellow onion, mince 3 garlic cloves, grate 3/8 cup Parmesan, and let 3/4 cup sour cream sit at room temperature for 20 to 30 minutes.
* Heat 1 tbsp olive oil in a large, high-walled skillet over medium heat. Add the diced 3/4 cup onion and minced 3 garlic cloves, cook for 5 minutes until softened, then transfer to a plate and let cool for 5 minutes.
* In a large bowl, combine the cooled onion-garlic mixture, 3/4 cup panko breadcrumbs, 3/8 cup Parmesan, 1 large beaten egg, 1/2 cup milk, 1 1/2 tsp kosher salt, 1/2 tsp dried oregano, 1/2 tsp ground allspice, 1/2 tsp ground nutmeg, and 1/2 tsp black pepper.
* Add 1 1/2 lb ground beef and 1 lb ground pork, then gently mix just until combined so the meatballs stay tender.
* Roll the mixture into about 32 meatballs, each about 1 1/2 inches wide, place them on a tray, and refrigerate for 15 minutes.
* In a large measuring cup, whisk together 3 cups beef broth, 1 1/2 cubes chicken bouillon, 3 tsp Worcestershire sauce, 1 1/2 tsp Dijon mustard, and 1 1/2 tsp dried parsley, then set aside.
* Heat 5 tbsp olive oil in the same skillet over medium-high heat. Brown the meatballs in batches for about 1 minute per side until a crust forms, then transfer each batch to a clean plate.
* Reduce heat to medium, melt 6 tbsp butter in the skillet, sprinkle in 6 tbsp flour, and whisk continuously for 2 minutes until lightly golden.
* Slowly pour in the prepared broth mixture in small splashes, whisking constantly until smooth, then bring to a gentle simmer.
* Place the room-temperature 3/4 cup sour cream in a medium bowl, whisk in 1/4 cup of the warm sauce until smooth, then whisk in another 1/4 cup warm sauce to fully temper it.
* Lower heat to low and whisk the tempered sour cream mixture back into the skillet until the gravy is smooth and creamy.
* Return all browned meatballs and any resting juices to the skillet, spoon gravy over the tops, partially cover, and simmer on low for 10 to 15 minutes until the centers reach 165 F.
* Taste the sauce and adjust with a pinch of salt or pepper if needed, then garnish with chopped parsley and serve with mashed potatoes or egg noodles.
