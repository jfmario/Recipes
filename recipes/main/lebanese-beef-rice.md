# Lebanese Beef and Rice

This is a one-pot rice pilaf style dish: spiced ground beef, fragrant rice, and a crown of butter-toasted nuts. Begin about 45 minutes before you want to eat. Have your seven spice blend already mixed (see the Seven Spice ingredient recipe); this card only uses it as a pantry item.

## Ingredients

* 2 lb ground beef
* 3 cups white basmati rice
* 4 1/2 cups low-sodium chicken or beef broth
* 6 tbsp unsalted butter
* 4 tsp seven spice blend (from your prepared batch)
* 1/2 cup slivered almonds
* 1/2 cup raw cashews, halved
* 6 tbsp pine nuts (optional)
* 2 cups plain whole-milk yogurt, for serving

## Steps

* Set out a large heavy pot with a tight lid (for the rice and beef), a skillet at least 10 inches wide (for the nuts), a fine-mesh strainer, a wooden spoon, measuring cups and spoons, and a large serving platter.
* Rinse 3 cups white basmati rice under cold water in the pot or a bowl, swishing and draining, until the water runs noticeably clearer (about 3 changes of water). Drain in the fine-mesh strainer and let stand at least 5 minutes so excess surface water drips away; leave the strainer in the sink or on a plate.
* Measure 1/2 cup slivered almonds, 1/2 cup halved raw cashews, and 6 tbsp pine nuts if you are using them. Line a dinner plate with a double layer of paper towels and keep it beside the stove for the nuts.
* Pour 2 tbsp neutral oil into the skillet and set over medium-high heat. When the oil shimmers after about 1 1/2 minutes, add all the measured nuts at once. Stir constantly with a heatproof spatula until the nuts are golden and smell toasted, about 2 1/2 minutes. Immediately scrape them onto the paper-towel plate and spread in a single layer; set aside.
* In a small bowl, combine 6 tbsp cold unsalted butter (cut into a few pieces if firm), 4 tsp seven spice blend, 1 tsp ground cinnamon, 2 tsp kosher salt, and 1 tsp freshly ground black pepper. Mash with a fork until the spices are evenly distributed through the butter. Set the bowl next to the stove.
* Set the large pot over medium heat. Crumble in 2 lb ground beef. Cook, breaking up clumps with the wooden spoon, until the meat is browned through and most released liquid has cooked off, about 8 to 12 minutes depending on how lean the beef is.
* Add the entire butter-and-spice mixture to the pot. Cook, stirring constantly, until the butter melts and the spices smell fragrant and a shade deeper, about 2 minutes.
* Add the drained 3 cups rinsed rice and 4 1/2 cups low-sodium chicken or beef broth. Raise heat to medium-high and bring to a full boil, stirring once or twice so nothing sticks.
* As soon as the liquid boils, reduce heat to medium-low, cover the pot tightly, and cook without lifting the lid for 15 minutes, or until the broth is absorbed and the rice grains are tender. If the liquid is gone but the rice is still firm in the center, drizzle in 2 tbsp hot water, re-cover, and steam another 3 to 5 minutes.
* Remove the pot from the heat and let stand covered 5 minutes. Fluff the rice gently with the fork, trying not to mash the grains.
* Turn the rice out onto the large platter and scatter all the toasted nuts evenly over the top. Bring 2 cups plain yogurt to the table in a bowl for spooning alongside each portion. Refrigerate leftovers in a sealed container and eat within 3 days; reheat with a splash of broth and a loose cover in the microwave or a covered skillet over low heat.
