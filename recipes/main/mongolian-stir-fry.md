# Mongolian Stir Fry

This Mongolian stir fry is scaled for 8 servings and keeps the classic takeout flavor: tender beef, caramelized soy-brown sugar sauce, and fresh green onion.

For the best texture, slice the beef thinly against the grain and cook it in batches so it sears instead of steaming.

## Ingredients

* 2 lb flank steak
* 2/3 cup cornstarch
* 1 cup low-sodium soy sauce
* 1/2 cup water
* 1 cup packed brown sugar
* 4 green onions
* 1/4 cup neutral oil, divided

## Steps

* Place 2 lb flank steak in the freezer for 15 to 20 minutes to firm it slightly, then slice it thinly (about 1/4 inch) against the grain.
* Trim and slice 4 green onions, mince 8 garlic cloves, and grate or finely mince 1 tsp fresh ginger so all aromatics are ready before cooking.
* Add the sliced 2 lb flank steak and 2/3 cup cornstarch to a large bowl and toss until all pieces are lightly coated, then shake off excess cornstarch.
* Heat 2 tsp of the 1/4 cup neutral oil in a large skillet over medium-low heat, then add the minced 8 garlic cloves and 1 tsp fresh ginger and cook for 30 to 60 seconds until fragrant.
* Add 1 cup low-sodium soy sauce, 1/2 cup water, and 1 cup packed brown sugar to the skillet, bring to a boil, and cook for 3 to 5 minutes until slightly thickened, then pour the sauce into a bowl and set aside.
* Return the skillet to medium-high heat and add 2 tbsp of the 1/4 cup neutral oil. Cook half of the coated beef in a single layer for about 2 minutes, turning once, just until browned, then transfer to a plate.
* Add the remaining oil (about 1 tbsp) and cook the second half of the coated beef for about 2 minutes, turning once, then transfer it to the same plate.
* Return all browned beef to the skillet, pour in the reserved sauce, and cook over medium heat for 2 to 3 minutes until the sauce is bubbling and evenly coats the beef.
* Turn off the heat, stir in the sliced 4 green onions, and serve immediately.
