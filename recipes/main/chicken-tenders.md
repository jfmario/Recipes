# Chicken Tenders

These chicken tenders keep the original seasoned flour-and-egg dredge, but use an air fryer and a light oil spray instead of deep frying. Plan for about 20 minutes of hands-on prep, 20 minutes of chilling time, and 25 to 30 minutes of air-frying in batches. For even cooking, avoid crowding the basket and keep strips similar in thickness.

## Ingredients

* 8 boneless, skinless chicken breasts
* 4 cups all-purpose flour
* 3 large eggs
* 2 3/4 cups whole milk
* 2/3 cup hot sauce (optional, for buffalo-style tenders)
* 2 1/2 tbsp unsalted butter (optional, for buffalo-style tenders)

## Steps

* Set out an air fryer, two large shallow bowls, one rimmed sheet pan, parchment paper, tongs, and measuring cups and spoons. Preheat the air fryer to 390 F for 5 minutes.
* Slice all 8 chicken breasts into strips about 1/2 inch thick, aiming for pieces 4 to 5 inches long so they cook at the same rate.
* In the first bowl, whisk together 4 cups all-purpose flour, 2 tbsp plus 2 tsp kosher salt, 2 tsp black pepper, 2 tsp cayenne pepper, 2 tsp paprika, and 1 1/2 tsp garlic powder until evenly combined.
* In the second bowl, whisk 3 large eggs with 2 3/4 cups whole milk until fully smooth.
* Line the sheet pan with parchment. Dip each chicken strip into the egg-milk mixture, let excess drip off, then coat in the seasoned flour. Dip the floured strip back into the egg-milk mixture and coat again in the seasoned flour to create a double coat; place each finished strip on the lined pan.
* Refrigerate the full tray of breaded chicken strips for 20 minutes so the coating hydrates and adheres.
* Remove the tray from the refrigerator. Lightly spray both sides of each breaded strip with neutral oil spray (about 2 to 3 seconds of spray per side, just enough to moisten the flour without soaking it).
* Arrange a single layer of chicken strips in the air fryer basket with a little space between pieces. Air-fry at 390 F for 6 minutes.
* Open the basket, lightly spray any dry floury spots with a quick burst of oil spray, flip each strip, then air-fry at 390 F for 5 to 7 minutes more, until the coating is golden and the thickest strips reach 165 F internally.
* Transfer cooked strips to a clean tray and repeat with remaining chicken, using the same 390 F temperature and 11 to 13 minutes total per batch.
* If making buffalo-style tenders, melt 2 1/2 tbsp unsalted butter in a small saucepan over low heat, then whisk in 2/3 cup hot sauce until glossy and hot, about 1 minute.
* For plain tenders, serve immediately. For buffalo tenders, toss only the portion you want sauced in the hot sauce-butter mixture and serve right away so the coating stays crisp.
