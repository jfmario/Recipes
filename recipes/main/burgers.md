# Grilled Burgers with Bacon

This is written for eight full-sized burgers on a charcoal or gas grill. Patties include finely diced onion and dry spices so they stay savory on the grate; handle them gently and only flip once for the best crust. If you prefer softer onion inside the meat, sweat the diced onion in a skillet with a splash of oil until translucent, cool completely, then mix into the beef. Check bacon and bun labels if you need to avoid gluten or specific allergens.

## Ingredients

* 3 lb ground beef (about 80/20 lean to fat)
* 2 medium yellow onions
* 1/4 cup panko breadcrumbs
* 1 lb thick-cut bacon
* 8 hamburger buns
* 8 slices cheddar or American cheese
* 1 small head iceberg lettuce
* 2 large ripe tomatoes
* 1 (16 oz) jar dill pickle chips

## Steps

* Read through the full sequence, then set out a large mixing bowl, a baking sheet lined with parchment or wax paper, a plate for shaped patties, tongs, a wide spatula, a small bowl for butter, and paper towels. If you are using a gas grill, preheat all burners on high with the lid closed for 10 to 15 minutes until the grates are very hot; if you are using charcoal, light a full chimney of coals and spread them in an even layer when covered with gray ash. Plan to cook bacon before the patties so the grill or kitchen is free for burgers at peak heat.
* Peel and trim 2 medium yellow onions. Finely dice enough of one onion to fill about 1 cup for the meat mixture (roughly half to two-thirds of one onion, depending on size). Slice the remaining onion and the second onion into thin rings for optional raw topping; set the rings aside in a covered container in the refrigerator if you are not using them for an hour or more.
* Crumble 3 lb ground beef into the large mixing bowl. Add the 1 cup finely diced onion, 1/4 cup panko breadcrumbs, 2 tbsp Worcestershire sauce, 2 tsp kosher salt, 1 tsp freshly ground black pepper, 1 tsp garlic powder, 1/2 tsp onion powder, and 1/2 tsp smoked paprika. Mix with your hands using a light touch until the onion and crumbs disappear into the meat without overworking it, about 30 seconds to 1 minute total.
* Divide the mixture into 8 equal portions about 6 oz each. Gently form each portion into a patty about 4 1/2 inches wide and slightly thicker in the center than at the edges; press a shallow dimple into the center of each patty with your thumb so they cook evenly. Arrange the patties on the lined baking sheet, cover loosely with plastic wrap, and refrigerate while you cook the bacon and prepare the buns and vegetables, at least 20 minutes and up to 4 hours.
* Cook 1 lb thick-cut bacon in whichever way fits your setup. For the oven, heat the oven to 400 F, lay the bacon in a single layer on a rimmed baking sheet (use a wire rack on the sheet if you have one for crisper strips), and bake until deeply browned and crisp, about 18 to 22 minutes, rotating the sheet once. For the stovetop, work in batches in a large skillet over medium heat until each batch is crisp, pouring off fat between batches if it pools more than about 1/8 inch. For the grill, use a cast-iron skillet or griddle on the grate over medium heat and cook in batches, lowering heat if the bacon smokes heavily. Drain all cooked bacon on paper towels; when cool enough to handle, break or cut strips in half if you want two shorter pieces per burger.
* While the bacon cooks, pull 8 leaves from 1 small head iceberg lettuce, rinse, and pat very dry. Core 2 large ripe tomatoes and cut into 1/4-inch slices; season the slices lightly with a pinch of salt on both sides if you will serve them within 30 minutes (otherwise wait until assembly). Open 1 (16 oz) jar dill pickle chips and drain off excess brine; set pickles, lettuce, tomatoes, and 8 slices cheddar or American cheese near your serving area.
* Split 8 hamburger buns and spread the cut sides very lightly with softened butter, using about 2 tbsp butter total across all bun halves if you like a thin even layer. When the grill is still hot from preheating or after bacon is done, toast the buns cut side down directly on the grates over medium heat until golden with char marks, about 1 to 2 minutes; watch closely and move any pieces that darken too fast. If the grill is tied up, toast the buns in a large dry skillet or under the broiler instead until golden. Set the toasted buns cut side up on a platter.
* Scrape the grill grates clean with a grill brush. Fold a paper towel into a pad, grasp with tongs, dip in a high-heat cooking oil, and rub the grates until lightly glossy. For gas, set the main cooking area to medium-high; for charcoal, you want a zone that feels very hot when you hold your hand 5 inches above for only 2 to 3 seconds.
* Season the top side of each chilled patty with a small pinch of kosher salt. Lay patties salted side down on the oiled grates without crowding; if needed, cook in two waves. Close the lid if using gas. Cook without pressing or moving until the bottom is deeply browned and the meat releases cleanly when nudged with the spatula, about 4 minutes for medium-rare to medium on a hot grill. Flip once, season the second side with another small pinch of salt, and cook about 3 to 4 minutes more for medium, or until an instant-read thermometer inserted horizontally into the center of a patty reads about 135 F for medium-rare or 145 F for medium; add 1 minute after flipping if you want cheese melted on the patty, laying 1 slice cheese on each patty in the last minute and closing the lid to help it soften.
* Transfer cooked patties to a clean platter and let rest 3 minutes so juices settle. Build each burger on the bottom bun with 1 patty (cheese side up if used), 2 half-strips or 1 full strip of bacon broken to fit, 1 lettuce leaf, 1 or 2 tomato slices, a few pickle chips, and optional raw onion rings. Spread the cut side of each top bun with ketchup, yellow mustard, or mayonnaise as you like, using about 1 tsp to 1 tbsp per bun depending on taste, then crown the burgers and serve immediately while the bacon is still crisp and the buns are warm.
