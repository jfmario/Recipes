# French Roast Sandwiches

This is a classic slow-cooker roast beef sandwich dinner built around a deeply savory cooking liquid that doubles as the dipping sauce. Start at least 8 hours before serving so the roast has enough time to become fork-tender.

## Ingredients

* 4 lb chuck roast or rump roast
* 21 oz condensed beef broth (two 10.5 oz cans)
* 10.5 oz condensed French onion soup (one can)
* 1 medium onion
* 2 garlic cloves
* 1 sprig fresh rosemary
* 1 tsp Worcestershire sauce
* 8 sub rolls
* 4 tbsp unsalted butter

## Steps

* Set out a slow cooker, a large skillet, tongs, a cutting board, and a knife. Slice 1 medium onion and mince 2 garlic cloves before you start browning the meat.
* Pat 1 4 lb chuck roast or rump roast dry, then season all sides generously with salt and black pepper.
* Heat the skillet over medium-high heat with a small drizzle of oil. Sear the roast for 2 to 3 minutes per side until all sides are deeply browned, then transfer the roast to the slow cooker.
* Pour in 21 oz condensed beef broth and 10.5 oz condensed French onion soup. Add the sliced onion, minced garlic, 1 tsp Worcestershire sauce, and 1 sprig fresh rosemary.
* Cover and cook on low for 8 hours, until the beef is very tender and shreds easily with a fork.
* Remove the cooked roast from the slow cooker and set it on a cutting board. Discard the rosemary sprig, then skim excess fat from the surface of the cooking liquid if needed.
* Preheat the broiler to high. Split 8 sub rolls, spread 1/2 tbsp unsalted butter on each roll, and place them cut-side up on a sheet pan.
* Broil the buttered rolls for 1 to 2 minutes until golden at the edges, watching closely so they do not burn.
* Thinly slice or shred the beef, then divide it among the toasted rolls.
* Ladle some hot cooking liquid over the beef on each sandwich to moisten, and serve immediately with the remaining hot au jus on the side for dipping.
