# Hibachi Steak

This is a fast, high-heat hibachi-style steak built from overnight-marinated New York strip.

Start the marinade the night before so the steak fully absorbs the soy, garlic, and honey mixture.

## Ingredients

* 3 lb New York strip steak
* 4 tbsp soy sauce
* 4 tbsp neutral oil, plus 2 tbsp for cooking
* 8 garlic cloves
* 2 tbsp ginger paste (optional)
* 2 tbsp Worcestershire sauce
* 4 tbsp honey
* Yum yum sauce, for serving (optional)

## Steps

* The night before cooking, mince 8 garlic cloves and add them to a large bowl with 4 tbsp soy sauce, 4 tbsp neutral oil, 2 tbsp Worcestershire sauce, and 4 tbsp honey; if using it, add 2 tbsp ginger paste.
* Whisk the marinade until smooth and fully combined.
* Cut 3 lb New York strip steak into roughly 1-inch cubes, add the cubes to the marinade, and toss until all pieces are evenly coated.
* Cover the bowl and refrigerate the marinated steak for at least 8 hours and up to 24 hours.
* When ready to cook, remove the steak from the refrigerator and let it sit at room temperature for 20 minutes while you preheat a large wok or skillet over high heat.
* Add 2 tbsp neutral oil to the hot pan, then add all marinated steak cubes in a single layer, leaving behind excess liquid if needed to prevent steaming.
* Sear and stir-fry the steak over high heat for about 4 to 6 minutes, stirring frequently, until the outside is browned and the center reaches your preferred doneness.
* Taste one piece and season with a pinch of kosher salt and black pepper only if needed, then serve immediately, optionally with yum yum sauce.
* If serving with the existing egg fried rice recipe, fold the cooked steak into the finished rice as an additional ingredient just before serving.
