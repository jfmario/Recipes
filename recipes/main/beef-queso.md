# Beef Queso

This version is written for 8 generous appetizer portions as a dip (or fewer people if it is the centerpiece of nachos). The dip comes together on the stove in one skillet; keep it warm over low heat or transfer to a slow cooker on warm if you are holding it for a party. Check your taco seasoning label if you need to avoid gluten or specific allergens; brands vary.

## Ingredients

* 1 lb ground beef
* 1 (10 oz) can diced tomatoes with green chiles
* 8 oz process cheese loaf (such as Velveeta)
* 2 cups shredded cheddar cheese
* 1/2 cup milk

## Steps

* Set a large skillet on the counter and gather a wooden spoon or heatproof spatula. Open 1 (10 oz) can diced tomatoes with green chiles, pour the contents into a strainer over the sink, and let the liquid drain while you shake the strainer gently once or twice. Cut 8 oz process cheese into rough 1/2-inch cubes. If you are not using pre-shredded cheese, shred enough cheddar to measure 2 cups.
* Crumble 1 lb ground beef into the skillet and set it over medium-high heat. Cook, breaking up the meat with the spoon, until no pink remains and the beef is lightly browned in spots, about 6 to 8 minutes. If more than a thin layer of fat pools in the pan, tilt the skillet and spoon off the excess so the dip does not taste greasy.
* Sprinkle 2 tbsp taco seasoning over the beef and stir for about 1 minute until the spices smell toasted and the meat is evenly coated.
* Reduce the heat to medium. Add the drained tomatoes, the cubed process cheese, and 2 cups shredded cheddar. Stir until the cheeses begin to melt, then pour in 1/2 cup milk. Continue stirring until the mixture is smooth and completely melted; if the bottom scorches, lower the heat to medium-low. Taste and adjust salt only if needed (many taco seasonings are already salty). Serve immediately while hot, with tortilla chips or over nachos. If you like, top with sliced jalapeños, chopped cilantro, or a spoonful of salsa.
