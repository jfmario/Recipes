# Dill Salmon

This salmon is seasoned in two stages: a short dill-salt cure, then a quick bake.

Begin about 1 hour before you plan to serve so the fish has time to cure and rest.

## Ingredients

* 4 lb salmon fillet, skin on
* 1/2 cup finely chopped fresh dill
* 3 lemons
* 2 tbsp olive oil

## Steps

* About 1 hour before cooking, finely chop enough dill to make 1/2 cup and zest 1 lemon if you want a brighter dill mixture.
* In a small bowl, combine 1/2 cup chopped dill with 2 tbsp kosher salt, then rub the mixture between your fingers for 1 minute to bruise the dill and release its oils.
* Line a baking sheet with parchment paper, place the 4 lb salmon fillet skin-side down, and rub the dill-salt mixture evenly over the top surface.
* Refrigerate the salmon on the baking sheet for 45 minutes.
* When the cure time is almost done, preheat the oven to 350F.
* Remove the salmon from the refrigerator, rinse off the dill-salt coating under cold water, and pat the fish very dry with paper towels.
* Return the salmon to the parchment-lined baking sheet, rub the top with 2 tbsp olive oil, and season evenly with 1 tsp freshly ground black pepper.
* Bake at 350F for 10 to 15 minutes, until the thickest part flakes easily and reaches your preferred doneness.
* While the salmon bakes, cut the remaining 3 lemons into wedges.
* Remove the salmon from the oven, loosely tent it with foil, and rest it for 8 minutes before serving with the lemon wedges.
