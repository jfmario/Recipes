# Carne Asada Tacos

These carne asada tacos are built around a bold lime, garlic, and cilantro marinade and a fast, high-heat grill. The steak is sliced after a short rest, then layered with creamy avocado and fresh toppings.

**Start the night before:** marinate the steak overnight for the best flavor and tenderness (minimum 4 hours if needed).

## Ingredients

* 3 lb flank steak
* 2/3 cup olive oil
* 8 limes
* 2 cups fresh cilantro, chopped
* 8 garlic cloves, minced
* 2 tsp ground cumin
* 1 tsp chili powder
* 1 tsp black pepper
* salt
* 4 avocados
* 1 medium white onion, finely chopped
* 16 corn or flour tortillas
* 2 cups shredded Monterey Jack cheese

## Steps

* The night before (or at least 4 hours ahead), chop 2 cups cilantro, mince 8 garlic cloves, and juice 6 limes (about 3/4 cup juice). In a large bowl, whisk together 2/3 cup olive oil, the 3/4 cup lime juice, 1 cup chopped cilantro, 8 minced garlic cloves, 2 tsp ground cumin, 1 tsp chili powder, 1 1/2 tsp salt, and 1 tsp black pepper.
* Add the 3 lb flank steak to the marinade and turn to coat all sides. Cover and refrigerate overnight, or refrigerate for at least 4 hours if you are short on time.
* About 40 minutes before serving, remove the marinated steak from the refrigerator so it can lose some chill before grilling.
* While the steak warms slightly, finely chop 1 medium white onion (about 2/3 cup), chop the remaining 1 cup cilantro, mash 4 avocados with 1/2 tsp salt and 2 tbsp lime juice, and cut the remaining 2 limes into wedges for serving.
* Preheat a grill or grill pan to medium-high heat. Lightly oil the grates, remove the steak from the marinade, and let excess marinade drip off.
* Grill the flank steak for about 5 minutes on the first side and 5 minutes on the second side, until nicely charred and the thickest part reaches 130-135 F for medium-rare.
* Transfer the steak to a cutting board and rest it for 5 minutes. During the rest, warm 16 corn or flour tortillas on the grill for 20-30 seconds per side, then keep them wrapped in a clean towel.
* Slice the rested steak thinly against the grain, then cut long slices into bite-size strips.
* Assemble tacos in this order for each tortilla: add a portion of sliced steak, about 2 tbsp mashed avocado, about 2 tbsp shredded Monterey Jack, about 1 tbsp chopped onion, and about 1 tbsp chopped cilantro. Serve immediately with lime wedges for squeezing.
