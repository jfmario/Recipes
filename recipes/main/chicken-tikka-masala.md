# Chicken Tikka Masala

This version is written for 8 servings. Chicken is browned whole, then cut so it stays juicy in the sauce. Full-fat coconut milk gives a gentle sweetness that pairs with the spice blend. Serve with steamed rice; warm naan is optional.

If the skillet is crowded when browning the chicken, work in two batches and use a little extra ghee for the second batch so each piece browns rather than steams.

## Ingredients

* 3 lb skinless boneless chicken thighs
* 2 medium yellow onions
* 1/2 cup tomato paste
* 8 garlic cloves
* 2 tbsp finely grated fresh ginger
* 2 cups canned crushed tomatoes
* 2 (13 oz) cans full-fat coconut milk
* 1 cup chicken broth
* 1/4 cup chopped fresh cilantro

## Steps

* Chop 2 medium yellow onions. Finely grate or mince 8 garlic cloves and finely grate 2 tbsp fresh ginger; keep the garlic and ginger together in a small dish. Set a large plate beside the stove for browned chicken.
* Trim excess fat from 3 lb skinless boneless chicken thighs if you like, then place the chicken in a large bowl. Drizzle 2 tbsp vegetable oil over the chicken and toss until evenly coated.
* In a small bowl, whisk together 4 tsp kosher salt, 4 tsp garam masala, 4 tsp ground cumin, 2 tsp ground coriander, 2 tsp smoked paprika, 2 tsp ground turmeric, 1 tsp ground black pepper, 1/2 tsp cayenne pepper, and 1/4 tsp ground cardamom. Sprinkle the mixture over the chicken and turn until every piece is evenly coated.
* Melt 2 tbsp clarified butter (ghee) in a large, heavy skillet over high heat. Add about half the chicken thighs in a single layer without crowding and cook until deeply browned on both sides, about 5 to 8 minutes total depending on thickness. Transfer that batch to the plate. Melt another 2 tbsp ghee in the skillet, brown the remaining chicken the same way, and transfer it to the plate. When the chicken is cool enough to handle, cut it into bite-sized pieces and pour any resting juices into a small bowl; reserve the juices for the sauce.
* Return the skillet with the browned drippings to medium-high heat. Add the chopped onion and sauté, stirring often, until softened and translucent, about 5 to 6 minutes.
* Stir in 1/2 cup tomato paste and cook, stirring constantly, until the paste darkens slightly and smells toasted, about 4 to 5 minutes. Add the prepared garlic and ginger and cook for about 1 minute, stirring, until fragrant.
* Pour in 2 cups crushed tomatoes and raise the heat. Bring to a boil while scraping the bottom of the skillet with a wooden spoon to dissolve any browned bits. Pour in both cans of coconut milk and 1 cup chicken broth, stir once, then bring to a lively simmer. Reduce heat to medium-low and cook uncovered, stirring occasionally, until the sauce thickens slightly and the flavors meld, about 15 minutes.
* Stir in the cut chicken along with any reserved juices from the plate and bowl. Add 1/4 cup chopped fresh cilantro and 1 tsp red pepper flakes. Return to a simmer, reduce heat to medium-low, and cook until the chicken is cooked through and tender, about 10 to 15 minutes, stirring occasionally.
* Taste the sauce and adjust with fine salt and additional ground black pepper if needed, then serve hot over rice.
