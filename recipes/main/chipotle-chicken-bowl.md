# Chipotle Chicken Bowl

This is written for 8 full bowls and combines a citrus–vinegar chicken marinade with stovetop or grill cooking, warm black beans, two cold salsas, guacamole, shredded lettuce, cheese, and sour cream. Plan at least 1 hour of refrigerator time for the chicken marinade (up to 8 hours is fine).

Cilantro Lime Rice: Serve these bowls with Cilantro Lime Rice from this collection (one full batch there is already scaled for 8 side portions). When to start it: If you use a 1-hour chicken marinade, begin Cilantro Lime Rice in the last 30 to 35 minutes of marinating so the rice finishes around the time you start cooking the chicken. If you marinate longer, you can cook Cilantro Lime Rice anytime in the last hour before dinner and hold it covered, or start it when you preheat the skillet or grill for the chicken so rice and protein finish together. For a short same-day window, start the rice first (it needs about 30 minutes from chopping cilantro to fluffing), then marinate the chicken while the rice rests covered if needed.

## Ingredients

* About 2 1/2 lb boneless skinless chicken breasts
* 24 oz frozen yellow sweet corn
* 6 ripe Hass avocados
* About 3 lb ripe tomatoes (for example 5 to 6 medium)
* 2 (15 oz) cans black beans
* 2 large hearts romaine lettuce (enough to shred about 8 cups)
* 8 oz Monterey Jack cheese
* 1 cup sour cream
* 2 to 3 medium red onions
* 1 large bunch fresh cilantro
* About 9 to 10 jalapeño peppers (use fewer or seed well for milder heat; not all need to be large)
* 6 limes
* 2 lemons

## Steps

### Chicken: prep and marinade

* On a cutting board, slice each chicken breast in half horizontally so you have thin cutlets about 1/2 inch thick; if any piece is still thick, cover with plastic and pound gently to even thickness.
* In a small bowl, whisk 4 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil with 2 tbsp distilled white vinegar (or substitute 2 tbsp fresh lime juice from about 2 limes), 2 tsp chili powder, 2 tsp dried oregano, 2 tsp smoked paprika, 2 tsp kosher salt, 1 tsp ground cumin, 1 tsp garlic powder, 1 tsp freshly ground black pepper, and 1 tsp cayenne pepper (use 1/2 tsp or omit for milder chicken).
* Place the chicken in a large zip-top bag, pour in the marinade, press out the air, seal, and massage to coat. Refrigerate at least 1 hour and up to 8 hours.

### Corn salsa (make during marinade or up to 1 day ahead)

* Set a colander in the sink, add 24 oz frozen yellow sweet corn, and rinse under cold water for about 1 minute, tossing with your hands; repeat once or twice after a short pause, then drain very well.
* Finely dice enough red onion to measure 1/2 cup. Seed and finely dice 2 medium jalapeño peppers (use 1 pepper or remove all seeds for mild salsa).
* Pull leaves from cilantro stems until you have 2/3 cup leaves, lightly packed, then chop.
* Juice 1 1/2 limes into a medium bowl. Add the drained corn, 1/2 cup diced red onion, the chopped jalapeños, and the 2/3 cup chopped cilantro. Add 1/2 tsp kosher salt, toss, taste, and adjust salt or lime. Cover and refrigerate if not using within 30 minutes.

### Fresh tomato salsa (pico; make during marinade or up to 1 day ahead)

* Core and dice about 3 lb ripe tomatoes into roughly 1/4-inch pieces; you should have about 6 cups diced.
* Finely dice more red onion until you have 1/2 cup total for this salsa (slice a second onion if needed).
* Pull more cilantro leaves to 1/2 cup lightly packed, chop, and add to a bowl with the tomatoes and onion.
* Finely chop 6 jalapeño peppers (seed them for moderate heat, leave some seeds for hotter salsa) and add to the bowl.
* Squeeze 2 tbsp juice from 1 lemon and 2 tbsp juice from 1 lime over the mixture, add 1 tsp kosher salt, toss gently, taste, and adjust salt or citrus. Cover and refrigerate until serving.

### Guacamole (make close to serving)

* Halve and pit 6 ripe Hass avocados and scoop the flesh into a medium bowl.
* Squeeze 1 tbsp fresh lemon juice (from the remaining lemon) and 1 tbsp fresh lime juice (from about 1 lime) over the avocados and mash with a fork until mostly creamy with some texture.
* Finely dice 1/2 cup red onion (use what remains from the onions after the salsas). Pull cilantro leaves to 1/4 cup chopped. Finely dice 2 tbsp seeded jalapeño (about 1 large pepper).
* Add the onion, cilantro, jalapeño, and 1/4 tsp kosher salt to the bowl, stir gently, taste, and adjust salt. If holding, press plastic wrap directly on the surface and refrigerate up to a few hours.

### Cook the chicken

* Remove the chicken from the refrigerator and let excess marinade drip off; discard the bag and used marinade.
* Stovetop: Heat 2 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil in a large nonstick skillet over medium-high until shimmering. Add chicken in a single layer (work in batches if needed) and cook 4 minutes without moving. Flip and cook 3 to 4 minutes more until deeply browned and the thickest part reads 165°F on an instant-read thermometer; if cutlets are thicker than 1/2 inch, reduce heat to medium and cover to finish without burning.
* Grill: Oil the grates, preheat to medium, and grill the chicken about 4 minutes per side until 165°F in the center.
* Transfer cooked chicken to a cutting board, rest 5 minutes, then cut into bite-size cubes.

### Beans and cheese

* Lightly drain 2 (15 oz) cans black beans so some liquid remains. Warm them in a small saucepan over low heat for 8 to 10 minutes, stirring occasionally, until steaming.
* While the beans warm, shred 8 oz Monterey Jack to yield about 2 cups.
* Shred or thinly slice romaine to yield about 8 cups packed loosely.

### Assemble 8 bowls

* Divide Cilantro Lime Rice (one batch from that recipe) and the 8 cups shredded romaine across 8 large bowls as the base.
* Top each bowl with a portion of diced chicken, a spoonful of warm black beans, about 2 tbsp corn salsa, and about 2 tbsp fresh tomato salsa (adjust to taste).
* Add a dollop of sour cream (about 2 tbsp per bowl from the 1 cup total), about 1/4 cup guacamole per bowl, and 2 tbsp shredded Monterey Jack per bowl, distributing the 2 cups cheese evenly.
* Cut 2 limes into wedges and serve 1 wedge per bowl on the side. Offer extra corn salsa, tomato salsa, and hot sauce at the table if you like.
