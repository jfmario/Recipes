# Chicken Shawarma

Bold Middle Eastern spices, lemon, and olive oil turn everyday chicken into something you’d happily queue for. Marinate the night before; the yogurt sauce can also be made ahead so the day-of work is mostly searing and assembly.

**Start the night before:** Stage 1 (marinating the chicken) should be done the day prior. Stage 2 (yoghurt sauce) can be done the same night or the next day.

## Ingredients

* 3–4 boneless, skinless chicken breasts or thighs
* 2 large garlic cloves, minced (marinade)
* 2 tbsp ground coriander
* 2 tbsp ground cumin
* 2 tbsp ground cardamom
* 1 tsp ground cayenne pepper
* 4 tsp smoked paprika
* 4 tsp kosher salt
* Black pepper, to taste
* 4 tbsp fresh lemon juice
* 6 tbsp olive oil
* 2 cups Greek yogurt
* 2 garlic cloves, crushed (sauce)
* 2 tsp ground cumin (sauce)
* Lemon juice, salt, and black pepper, to taste (sauce)
* Flatbreads
* Lettuce, sliced, for serving
* Neutral oil, for the skillet

## Steps

### The Night Before (or Early Same Day)

* Mince 2 large garlic cloves. In a bowl, combine the minced garlic, 2 tbsp coriander, 2 tbsp cumin, 2 tbsp cardamom, 1 tsp cayenne, 4 tsp smoked paprika, 4 tsp salt, several grinds of black pepper, 4 tbsp lemon juice, and 6 tbsp olive oil. Stir until evenly mixed.
* Add the chicken to a zip-top bag or shallow covered dish, pour the marinade over it, and turn until fully coated. Refrigerate overnight.

### Yoghurt Sauce (Night Before or Day Of)

* In a bowl, stir together 2 cups Greek yogurt, 2 crushed garlic cloves, 2 tsp cumin, a squeeze of lemon juice, and salt and pepper to taste. Cover and refrigerate until serving.

### Cook and Serve

* Film a large skillet with a little neutral oil and set over medium-high heat. If you need two batches, plan accordingly — when you start the last batch, preheat the oven to 350°F to toast the flatbreads.
* Cook the chicken: about 4–5 minutes on the first side and 3–4 minutes on the second, until cooked through with good color. If the centers need more time, reduce heat and finish cooking, slicing partway through if that helps even cooking.
* Transfer cooked chicken to a plate and tent loosely with foil while you finish any remaining batches or prep the bread.
* Toast flatbreads in the oven for about 2 minutes until warm and slightly crisp at the edges.
* Slice the chicken if you have not already.
* For each plate: lay down a toasted flatbread, add sliced chicken, a generous spoon of yoghurt sauce, and sliced lettuce. Serve immediately.
