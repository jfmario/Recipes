# Gyros

These gyros are built for a smooth service: bake the meat loaves and make tzatziki the night before, then re-crisp sliced meat right before dinner for classic gyro texture.

**Start the night before:** Make the tzatziki and bake the gyro loaves in advance so the day-of work is mostly slicing, crisping, and assembling.

## Ingredients

* 2 lb ground lamb
* 2 lb ground beef (80/20)
* 3 medium onions
* 10 garlic cloves
* 1/2 cup gluten-free breadcrumbs
* 2 cups Greek yogurt
* 1 large cucumber
* 2 tbsp fresh lemon juice
* 2 tbsp fresh dill, finely chopped
* 1 romaine lettuce heart
* 16 pita breads

## Steps

### Night Before: Tzatziki

* Grate 1 large cucumber, toss it with a pinch of salt, and let it drain in a colander for 10 minutes. Wrap the cucumber in a clean towel and squeeze out as much liquid as possible.
* Mince 2 garlic cloves. In a bowl, combine the drained cucumber, 2 cups Greek yogurt, the minced garlic, 2 tbsp lemon juice, and 2 tbsp chopped dill. Season with salt and black pepper to taste, stir well, cover, and refrigerate overnight.

### Night Before: Gyro Loaves

* Preheat the oven to 325 F. Lightly oil 2 standard loaf pans.
* Mince 8 garlic cloves. Peel 2 onions, cut them into chunks, and pulse in a food processor until very finely chopped (not pureed).
* In a large bowl, combine the minced garlic, processed onions, 2 lb ground lamb, 2 lb ground beef, and 1/2 cup gluten-free breadcrumbs.
* Add 2 tbsp dried oregano, 4 tsp ground cumin, 2 tsp paprika, a pinch of cayenne pepper, 4 tsp kosher salt, and 1 tsp black pepper. Mix and knead firmly for 3-4 minutes until the mixture is dense and sticky.
* Divide the meat mixture evenly between the 2 loaf pans and press it down tightly so there are no air pockets.
* Bake at 325 F for 60 minutes, until fully cooked through.
* Let the loaves rest in the pans for 20 minutes. Transfer them to a plate, cover, and refrigerate overnight. Reserve the rendered fat from the pans in a container and refrigerate it for reheating the sliced meat.

### Day Of: Prep Components

* Remove the chilled gyro loaves, reserved rendered fat, and tzatziki from the refrigerator 30 minutes before cooking.
* Slice 1 romaine heart thinly and set aside.
* Peel and thinly slice the remaining 1 onion and set aside for serving.
* Slice each chilled gyro loaf into thin strips.

### Day Of: Crisp Meat and Warm Pitas

* Preheat the oven to 275 F. Arrange 16 pita breads on sheet pans so they can be warmed in 1-2 batches.
* Heat a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add a spoonful of reserved rendered fat (or a small splash of oil if needed).
* Cook the sliced gyro meat in batches in a single layer. For each batch, cook for about 1 minute 20 seconds on the first side, flip, spoon a little more reserved fat over the meat, and cook another 1 minute 20 seconds until browned and crisp at the edges.
* Transfer each cooked batch to a warm plate and repeat until all meat is crisped.
* During the final meat batch, warm the pitas in the 275 F oven for about 10 minutes.

### Serve

* To assemble each gyro, place warm meat in a pita, add a generous spoonful of tzatziki, then top with sliced romaine and sliced onion. Serve immediately.
