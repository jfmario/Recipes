# Chili Dogs

This recipe treats the chili as a hot dog topping, not a standalone bowl of chili. The meat is broken down to a fine crumb, there are no beans, and the finished sauce stays loose enough to spoon over the dog and seep slightly into the bun. If you prefer a thicker topping, simmer uncovered a little longer at the end until it matches your taste.

**Equipment note:** A food processor makes the fine beef texture predictable. If you do not have one, brown the beef as written, then press and chop it aggressively with a stiff spatula or potato masher while it cooks until the pieces are very small.

## Ingredients

* 1 1/2 lb ground beef (about 80/20 lean to fat)
* 8 beef hot dogs
* 8 hot dog buns
* 2 medium yellow onions
* 1 tbsp neutral cooking oil (such as vegetable or canola)
* 1 (6 oz) can tomato paste
* 3 cups low-sodium beef broth
* 2 tbsp chili powder
* 1 1/2 tsp ground cumin
* 1 tsp smoked paprika
* 1 tsp garlic powder
* 1 tsp onion powder
* 2 tbsp Worcestershire sauce
* 3 tbsp plus 2 tsp prepared yellow mustard (1 tbsp for the chili, about 1 tsp per dog for topping)
* 1 1/2 tsp kosher salt, plus up to 1/4 tsp more if the chili tastes flat at the end
* 1/2 tsp black pepper
* 1 tsp apple cider vinegar (optional, if the chili tastes heavy at the end)
* 1/2 tsp granulated sugar (optional, with the vinegar if you use it)

## Steps

* Read the sequence once, then set out a food processor (if using), a Dutch oven or deep skillet with a lid, a cutting board, a chef knife, a wooden spoon or stiff spatula, and a plate for the finished dogs. If you will steam the hot dogs, set a saucepan with a tight lid near the stove; if you will griddle them, set a skillet or griddle pan nearby.
* Pull 1 1/2 lb ground beef from the refrigerator. Working in two batches so the machine is never more than half full, pulse the cold beef in a food processor until it looks like fine crumbles with no large chunks remaining, about 8 to 10 one-second pulses per batch, scraping the bowl once between bursts. If you are skipping the processor, leave the beef in its original grind and plan to mash it fine while browning in a later step.
* Peel and trim 2 medium yellow onions. Finely dice 1 onion until you have about 1 cup of small even pieces for the chili base. Leave the second onion whole for now and set it aside; you will chop it for topping closer to serving.
* Set the Dutch oven over medium-high heat. When the pot feels hot, add 1 tbsp neutral cooking oil and swirl to coat. Add the prepared beef (processed or not) and the 1 cup diced onion. Cook, breaking up the meat with your spoon or spatula and stirring often, until the beef is browned and the onion is soft, about 8 to 10 minutes. If you skipped processing, spend extra time mashing and chopping motions with a potato masher until the beef is in very small pieces.
* Reduce heat to medium. Clear a small hot spot in the center, add the full 6 oz can tomato paste, and stir it against the bottom of the pot for about 2 minutes until it darkens slightly and smells less raw.
* Pour in 3 cups low-sodium beef broth while scraping up any browned bits. Stir in 2 tbsp chili powder, 1 1/2 tsp ground cumin, 1 tsp smoked paprika, 1 tsp garlic powder, 1 tsp onion powder, 2 tbsp Worcestershire sauce, 1 tbsp prepared yellow mustard, 1 1/2 tsp kosher salt, and 1/2 tsp black pepper. Bring to a lively simmer, then reduce heat to maintain a gentle simmer, partially cover with the lid ajar, and cook for 45 to 55 minutes, stirring every 10 to 15 minutes so the bottom does not scorch. The sauce should reduce until it is thick enough to coat a spoon but still loose enough to puddle slightly on a plate, not stiff like a meatloaf mixture.
* Taste the chili and adjust: if it tastes flat, add another 1/4 tsp salt; if it tastes heavy, stir in 1 tsp apple cider vinegar and 1/2 tsp granulated sugar, simmer 2 minutes, then taste again. Turn heat to low to keep the chili warm while you finish the dogs and onion.
* Peel the reserved second onion and chop it into pieces about 1/4 inch or smaller so you have roughly 1 cup of fresh chopped onion for topping. If you are sensitive to raw onion bite, you may rinse the chopped onion under cold water in a strainer for 10 seconds, then drain well.
* Warm 8 hot dog buns if you like: split them, lay them cut side up on a baking sheet, and run them under a broiler set to low until the cut faces are lightly toasted, about 1 to 2 minutes, watching constantly; alternatively, toast cut side down in a dry skillet over medium heat for about 1 minute per side.
* Cook 8 beef hot dogs using whichever method fits your kitchen. To steam, bring about 1 inch of water to a simmer in a saucepan, stand the dogs upright or lay them in a steamer basket above the water, cover, and steam until heated through, about 6 to 8 minutes. To griddle or skillet-cook, heat a dry skillet over medium, add the dogs, and turn every minute or two until browned and hot through, about 6 minutes total.
* Lay each toasted bun open. Nestle 1 hot dog in each bun. Spoon about 1/2 to 2/3 cup warm chili over each dog, enough to cover most of the frank without drowning the entire bun unless you prefer it that way. Squeeze about 1 tsp yellow mustard per dog over the chili if you want the classic sharp contrast, then scatter about 2 tbsp chopped raw onion over each. Serve immediately while the chili is still warm and the onion is crisp.
