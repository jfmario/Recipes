# Crispy Chicken Sandwiches

These sandwiches use the same seasoned flour, egg-milk wash, and double coat as the chicken tenders recipe, but the chicken is portioned and pounded into cutlets that cover the bun. An air fryer keeps the breading crisp with light oil spray instead of deep frying. Expect about 25 minutes of hands-on work, 20 minutes of chilling after breading, and 30 to 40 minutes of air frying in batches. Toast buns while the last batch of chicken cooks so everything is warm at assembly.

## Ingredients

* 3 lb boneless skinless chicken breast
* 3 cups all-purpose flour
* 3 large eggs
* 2 1/2 cups whole milk
* 8 sandwich buns (brioche, potato, or standard hamburger buns)
* 1 (16 oz) jar dill pickle chips
* 1 small head iceberg lettuce
* 2 large ripe tomatoes
* 8 slices American or cheddar cheese (optional)

## Steps

* Set out an air fryer, two large shallow bowls, one rimmed sheet pan, parchment paper, tongs, a meat mallet or heavy skillet for pounding, plastic wrap, and measuring cups and spoons. Preheat the air fryer to 390 F for 5 minutes.
* Trim 3 lb boneless skinless chicken breast of excess fat and tendons. Slice each breast horizontally through the middle where natural thickness allows, working toward 8 separate cutlets of roughly equal size (if a breast is small, leave it whole and pound it thin instead of splitting). Place each cutlet between two sheets of plastic wrap and pound to an even 1/2-inch thickness so pieces are large enough to sit on a bun without doming excessively.
* In the first bowl, whisk together 3 cups all-purpose flour, 2 tbsp kosher salt, 1 1/2 tsp black pepper, 1 1/2 tsp cayenne pepper, 1 1/2 tsp paprika, and 1 tsp garlic powder until evenly combined.
* In the second bowl, whisk 3 large eggs with 2 1/2 cups whole milk until fully smooth.
* Line the sheet pan with parchment. Dip each cutlet into the egg-milk mixture, let excess drip off, then coat in the seasoned flour. Dip the floured cutlet back into the egg-milk mixture and coat again in the seasoned flour for a double coat; place each finished cutlet on the lined pan without overlapping.
* Refrigerate the full tray of breaded chicken for 20 minutes so the coating hydrates and adheres.
* While the chicken chills, pull 8 large leaves from 1 small head iceberg lettuce, rinse, and pat very dry. Core 2 large ripe tomatoes and cut into 1/4-inch slices. Open 1 (16 oz) jar dill pickle chips and drain off excess brine. If using cheese, unwrap 8 slices American or cheddar and keep them cool until assembly.
* Remove the tray from the refrigerator. Lightly spray both sides of each breaded cutlet with neutral oil spray (about 2 to 3 seconds per side, enough to moisten the flour without soaking it).
* Arrange a single layer of cutlets in the air fryer basket with a little space between pieces. Air-fry at 390 F for 7 minutes.
* Open the basket, lightly spray any dry floury spots with a quick burst of oil spray, flip each cutlet, then air-fry at 390 F for 6 to 8 minutes more, until the coating is deep golden and the thickest part reads 165 F on an instant-read thermometer.
* Transfer cooked cutlets to a wire rack set over a sheet pan or a clean tray so steam does not soften the underside. Repeat with remaining chicken, keeping batches the same size so timing stays predictable.
* Split 8 sandwich buns. Spread the cut sides very lightly with softened butter if you like, using about 2 tbsp butter total across all halves. Toast cut side down in a large dry skillet over medium heat, on a griddle, or under the broiler until golden, about 1 to 2 minutes; watch closely. If you want cheese melted onto the chicken, lay 1 slice cheese on each hot cutlet during the last minute of its rest or return briefly to the air fryer at 350 F for 1 minute until the cheese softens.
* Build each sandwich on the bottom bun with 1 crispy cutlet (cheese side up if used), 4 to 6 dill pickle chips, 1 lettuce leaf, and 1 or 2 tomato slices. Spread the cut side of each top bun with mayonnaise, or a mix of mayonnaise and yellow mustard, using about 1 tbsp per bun depending on taste; add a few drops of hot sauce on the bun or pickles if you want heat. Crown the sandwiches and serve immediately while the breading is still crisp.
