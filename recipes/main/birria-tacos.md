# Birria Tacos

These birria tacos are written for 8 servings (about 16 tacos), with tender shredded beef and a rich dipping broth.

This is a long cook; complete all chopping and spice measuring before browning the chiles so the braise stays smooth and controlled.

## Ingredients

* 2 lb beef stew meat, cut into 1-inch pieces
* 4 dried guajillo chiles
* 2 dried ancho chiles
* 1 dried chile de arbol
* 1 large white onion
* 7 garlic cloves
* 2 cups beef broth
* 1 (14 oz) can diced tomatoes
* 1/4 cup distilled white vinegar
* 16 (6-inch) flour tortillas
* 4 cups shredded Oaxaca or Monterey Jack cheese
* 1/2 cup chopped fresh cilantro
* 2 limes

## Steps

* Remove stems and most seeds from 4 dried guajillo chiles, 2 dried ancho chiles, and 1 dried chile de arbol. Dice 1 large white onion and mince 7 garlic cloves. Measure 2 tsp sesame seeds, 2 tsp ground cumin, 1 tsp black peppercorns, 1 tsp coriander seeds, 1/2 inch cinnamon stick, 2 whole cloves, 3 bay leaves, 2 tsp dried Mexican oregano, 1/4 tsp dried thyme, and 2 tsp kosher salt into a small bowl.
* Heat 2 cups beef broth in a saucepan over low heat until steaming, then keep warm.
* Heat a large Dutch oven over medium heat for 1 minute. Add the measured sesame seeds, cumin, peppercorns, coriander seeds, cinnamon stick, cloves, and bay leaves; toast for 1 to 2 minutes, stirring constantly, until fragrant. Transfer to a blender.
* Add 1 tbsp neutral oil to the same pot over medium heat. Toast the prepared 4 guajillo chiles, 2 ancho chiles, and 1 chile de arbol for about 1 minute, turning often so they darken slightly without burning. Transfer the chiles to the blender.
* Add half of the diced onion, all 7 minced garlic cloves, 1 (14 oz) can diced tomatoes, 1/4 cup white vinegar, the warm 2 cups beef broth, 2 tsp oregano, 1/4 tsp thyme, and 2 tsp kosher salt to the blender. Blend until fully smooth, 1 to 2 minutes.
* Heat 1 tbsp neutral oil in the Dutch oven over medium-high heat. Add 2 lb beef stew meat and sear for 6 to 8 minutes, stirring occasionally, until browned on multiple sides.
* Add the remaining diced onion and cook with the beef for 3 minutes until slightly softened.
* Pour the blended chile sauce over the beef, stirring to coat all 2 lb meat. Bring to a gentle boil over medium-high heat, then reduce to low, cover, and simmer for 2 1/2 to 3 hours until the beef is fork-tender.
* Transfer the cooked beef to a bowl and shred it with 2 forks. Return shredded beef to the pot and stir it into the braising liquid. Skim excess fat from the surface and reserve about 1 cup of the red fat for frying tacos.
* Finely chop 1/2 cup cilantro and cut 2 limes into wedges. Keep the remaining consommé warm on low heat for dipping.
* Heat a large nonstick or cast-iron skillet over medium heat. Dip 1 flour tortilla lightly into the reserved birria fat, place it in the skillet, and sprinkle 1/4 cup shredded Oaxaca or Monterey Jack cheese over half the tortilla.
* Add about 1/4 cup shredded birria beef on top of the cheese, fold the tortilla, and cook for 2 to 3 minutes per side until crisp and deeply golden. Repeat with the remaining 15 tortillas, 3 3/4 cups cheese, and remaining beef, adding more reserved fat to the skillet as needed.
* Serve the tacos hot with warm consommé for dipping, plus the chopped 1/2 cup cilantro and lime wedges from 2 limes.
