# King Pao Chicken

This King Pao Chicken is a fast, high-heat stir fry with bold takeout-style flavor: savory soy, bright vinegar, balanced sweetness, and the signature tingle of Sichuan pepper.

For the best texture, have everything measured, chopped, and ready before the wok goes on the heat. At this batch size, cooking in two rounds keeps the chicken seared instead of steamed.

## Ingredients

* 2 lb (1 kg) boneless, skinless chicken thighs, cut into bite-size pieces
* 1 tbsp + 1 tsp cornstarch
* 2 tbsp light soy sauce
* 3 tbsp dark soy sauce
* 1/4 cup Chinese black vinegar
* 2 tbsp Shaoxing wine
* 6 tbsp sugar
* 1 tsp sesame oil
* 2/3 cup water
* 1/4 cup peanut oil (or other neutral high-heat oil)
* 4 garlic cloves, minced
* 2 tsp fresh ginger, finely chopped
* 12 to 16 dried red chilies, cut into short pieces with most seeds discarded
* 6 green onions, cut into 3/4-inch pieces, whites and greens separated
* 1 tbsp ground Sichuan peppercorns, plus more to taste
* 1 1/2 cups roasted unsalted peanuts

## Steps

* If serving with rice, start it first so it is ready when the stir fry finishes.
* Place the 2 lb (1 kg) chicken thigh pieces in a bowl. In a second bowl, whisk 1 tbsp + 1 tsp cornstarch with 2 tbsp light soy sauce until smooth, then whisk in 3 tbsp dark soy sauce, 1/4 cup Chinese black vinegar, 2 tbsp Shaoxing wine, 6 tbsp sugar, and 1 tsp sesame oil.
* Pour 3 tbsp of the sauce mixture over the chicken and toss well. Let the chicken marinate for 15 to 20 minutes at room temperature. Whisk 2/3 cup water into the remaining sauce and set it beside the stove.
* Heat a wok or large skillet over high heat until very hot. Add 2 tbsp peanut oil, then add 2 minced garlic cloves, 1 tsp chopped ginger, and 6 to 8 dried chilies. Stir-fry for 30 seconds until fragrant.
* Add half of the marinated chicken and the white parts from 3 green onions. Stir-fry over high heat for 2 to 3 minutes, until the chicken is nearly cooked through.
* Re-whisk the sauce, then pour in half of it and add 1 1/2 tsp ground Sichuan peppercorns. Stir constantly for 1 to 2 minutes, until the sauce reduces to a thick, glossy syrup that coats the chicken.
* Add 3/4 cup peanuts and the green parts from 3 green onions. Toss for 20 to 30 seconds, transfer the first batch to a platter, and keep warm.
* Cook the second batch the same way: heat the remaining 2 tbsp peanut oil, then stir-fry the remaining 2 garlic cloves, 1 tsp ginger, and 6 to 8 dried chilies for 30 seconds. Add the remaining chicken and the white parts from the remaining 3 green onions; stir-fry for 2 to 3 minutes.
* Add the remaining sauce and the remaining 1 1/2 tsp ground Sichuan peppercorns, then stir for 1 to 2 minutes until syrupy. Finish with the remaining 3/4 cup peanuts and green parts from the remaining 3 green onions.
* Combine both batches on the platter, taste, and add an extra pinch of Sichuan peppercorns if you want more numbing heat. Serve immediately.
