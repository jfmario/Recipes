# Shrimp Pad Thai

This version follows the shrimp path from the source recipe: no chicken or tofu. The batch is doubled for eight servings, so use your largest wok or a deep skillet; if the pan feels crowded when you add the noodles and sauce, toss in two waves and combine on a platter. Have every component cut and the sauce mixed before you turn on the heat under the wok.

## Ingredients

* 1 lb flat rice noodles (pad thai width)
* 1 lb large raw shrimp, peeled and deveined, patted dry
* 6 garlic cloves
* 4 large eggs
* 2 cups fresh bean sprouts
* 2 red bell peppers, thinly sliced
* 6 green onions, thinly sliced on the bias, for garnish
* 1 cup dry roasted peanuts, divided for cooking and garnish
* 4 limes, cut into wedges
* 1 cup fresh cilantro leaves, roughly chopped
* 6 tbsp fish sauce
* 2 tbsp low-sodium soy sauce
* 10 tbsp packed light brown sugar
* 4 tbsp rice vinegar (or substitute 4 tbsp tamarind paste for a more traditional profile)
* 2 tbsp Sriracha, plus more to taste at the table
* 4 tbsp creamy peanut butter

## Steps

* Set a large pot of unsalted water over high heat and bring it to a boil while you prep, or soak the noodles per the package if they call for soaking instead of boiling.
* In a medium bowl, whisk 6 tbsp fish sauce, 2 tbsp low-sodium soy sauce, 10 tbsp packed light brown sugar, 4 tbsp rice vinegar, 2 tbsp Sriracha, and 4 tbsp creamy peanut butter until the sugar dissolves and the mixture is smooth. Set the bowl beside the stove.
* Mince 6 garlic cloves. Thinly slice 2 red bell peppers. Thinly slice 6 green onions on the bias. Roughly chop 1 cup cilantro. Cut 4 limes into wedges. Measure 2 cups bean sprouts. Crack 4 large eggs into a small bowl and beat them lightly with a fork. Pat 1 lb peeled and deveined shrimp very dry with paper towels.
* Cook 1 lb rice noodles according to the package until just tender (many brands need only a few minutes once boiling). Drain immediately, rinse under cold water until cool, and toss with 1 tsp neutral oil so they do not stick; set aside.
* Heat a wok or your largest skillet over medium-high heat until a drop of water sizzles. Add 4 tbsp neutral oil and swirl. Add all minced garlic and both thinly sliced red bell peppers; stir-fry 1 minute until fragrant and the peppers start to soften.
* Add the 1 lb raw shrimp in a single layer as much as possible. Cook 1 to 2 minutes per side until pink and just opaque; do not overcook.
* Push the shrimp and vegetables to one side of the pan. Add 2 tbsp more neutral oil to the empty side, pour in the beaten 4 eggs, and scramble, breaking them into small curds with a spatula until just set, 1 to 2 minutes, then fold them into the shrimp mixture.
* Add the drained noodles, the entire bowl of sauce, 2 cups bean sprouts, and about 3/4 cup of the 1 cup peanuts (reserve the rest for topping). Toss constantly until the noodles are hot through, the sauce coats everything, and the bean sprouts are slightly wilted, 2 to 4 minutes. If the pan is too full to toss cleanly, remove half to a bowl, toss the remainder, then return the first half and toss again until evenly combined.
* Transfer to a large platter or shallow bowls. Top with all 6 sliced green onions, the reserved 1/4 cup peanuts, the 1 cup chopped cilantro, and wedges from all 4 limes. Serve immediately, passing extra Sriracha at the table.
* Refrigerate leftovers in a sealed container and eat within 2 to 3 days; reheat in a hot skillet with 2 tbsp water, tossing until hot, to loosen the noodles.
