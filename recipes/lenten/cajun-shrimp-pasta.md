# Cajun Shrimp Pasta

This follows the same one-pan structure as Cajun Chicken Pasta, scaled for 8 servings: aromatics and pasta simmer in broth and tomatoes, then cream cheese finishes the sauce. Shrimp is seared first, set aside, and folded back in at the end so it does not turn rubbery during the long simmer. Use at least a 6-quart deep skillet or Dutch oven so everything fits; stir often while the pasta cooks.

If you want less heat, use 1/4 tsp cayenne in the spice mix instead of 1/2 tsp, or omit cayenne entirely.

## Ingredients

* 2 lb peeled and deveined shrimp (thawed if frozen), patted dry
* 2 medium yellow onions
* 1 lb dry penne pasta (or another short pasta shape)
* 2 (15 oz) cans fire roasted diced tomatoes
* 4 cups vegetable broth or seafood stock
* 4 oz cream cheese (half a standard brick)
* 6 green onions

## Steps

* Dice 2 medium yellow onions. In a small bowl, stir together 4 tsp smoked paprika, 2 tsp dried oregano, 2 tsp dried thyme, 1 tsp garlic powder, 1 tsp onion powder, 1/2 tsp cayenne pepper (use 1/4 tsp or omit for milder), 1/2 tsp black pepper, and 1/2 tsp salt to make the Cajun spice mix.
* Place 2 lb peeled and deveined shrimp in a bowl, add the spice mix, and toss until the shrimp are evenly coated.
* Set a large deep skillet or 6-quart Dutch oven over medium-high heat. Add 2 tbsp olive oil and 2 tbsp butter and heat until the pan is very hot and the butter is melted and foamy.
* Add the seasoned shrimp in a single layer where possible and cook about 1 to 2 minutes per side until they are pink at the edges and lightly colored; they should still look slightly underdone in the centers. Transfer the shrimp to a plate and set aside.
* Add the diced onions to the pan and sauté about 2 minutes until they begin to soften, scraping up any browned bits from the pan bottom with a wooden spoon.
* Add 1 lb dry penne, both cans of fire roasted diced tomatoes with their juices, and 4 cups vegetable broth or seafood stock. Stir once to combine, then cover and bring the liquid to a boil.
* When boiling, reduce heat to medium-low and simmer about 12 to 15 minutes, uncovering to stir every couple of minutes, until the penne is tender and the liquid is thick and saucy; if the pan runs dry before the pasta is done, add a splash more broth or water and continue simmering.
* Cut 4 oz cream cheese into small chunks, add to the skillet, and stir until it melts completely into the sauce.
* Return the reserved shrimp and any accumulated juices to the skillet, fold into the pasta, and cook over medium-low about 2 to 3 minutes, stirring gently, until the shrimp are opaque throughout and heated through.
* Thinly slice 6 green onions, scatter over the pasta, and serve hot.
