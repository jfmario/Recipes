# Chickpea Curry

This is a pantry-friendly lenten curry: onions and tomatoes cook down first, then chickpeas and spices simmer in coconut milk until the sauce is creamy. Use full-fat canned coconut milk for the intended richness; finish with fresh lime juice before serving — skipping it noticeably flattens the dish.

If you are serving rice or another starch, start it before the curry so both finish around the same time.

## Ingredients

* 4 tbsp coconut oil
* 2 medium red or yellow onions
* Two (14 to 15 oz / 400 g) cans diced tomatoes, with juices (or about 28 oz / 800 g fresh tomatoes, diced)
* Two (15 to 16 oz / 425 to 454 g) cans chickpeas
* Two (13.5 fl oz / 400 ml) cans full-fat coconut milk
* 2 small limes

## Steps

* Drain both cans of chickpeas into a colander and rinse under cold water until the water runs clear; shake off excess water and set aside. If you are serving with rice, start the rice now according to your usual method.
* Peel and dice 2 medium onions into roughly 1/4-inch pieces. Mince 6 garlic cloves and set the garlic beside the stove in a small dish.
* Set a deep pot or Dutch oven over medium-high heat. When the pot is hot, add 4 tbsp coconut oil and let it melt until it shimmers.
* Add the diced onions and both cans of diced tomatoes with their juices. Season with a generous pinch of salt and several grinds of black pepper, then stir. Reduce the heat to medium and cook, stirring occasionally, for about 10 minutes, until the tomatoes have released their liquid, the mixture has thickened slightly, and the onions are very soft.
* Add the rinsed chickpeas from two 15 to 16 oz (425 to 454 g) cans, the minced garlic, 3 tbsp garam masala, 2 tsp curry powder, and 1/2 tsp ground cumin. Stir until the chickpeas are evenly coated and the spices smell fragrant, about 1 minute.
* Shake both 13.5 fl oz (400 ml) cans of coconut milk well (fat often separates in the can), then pour them into the pot and stir to combine. If you want a slightly thicker sauce, whisk 4 tsp coconut flour (or another gluten-free flour) into a little of the hot liquid in a cup until smooth, then stir that slurry back into the pot; you may omit this step for a looser sauce.
* Bring the curry to a full boil, then reduce the heat to medium-low so it bubbles gently. Simmer uncovered for 10 to 12 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the flavors meld and the sauce is as thick as you like.
* Remove the pot from the heat. Taste and adjust with more salt and black pepper if needed. Squeeze in the juice of 2 small limes (fish out any seeds), stir well, and let stand 2 to 3 minutes before serving so the acidity mellows slightly. Serve hot with rice, flatbread, or as you prefer.
