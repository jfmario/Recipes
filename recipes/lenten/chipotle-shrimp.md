# Chipotle Shrimp

This dish builds a creamy chipotle sauce around shrimp for a rich but quick lenten-light main. The shrimp gets a short lime-water soak for cleaner flavor, then cooks briefly before simmering in sauce.

If you want rice with this meal, start White Basmati Rice first so both components finish at the same time.

## Ingredients

* 2 1/4 lb frozen shrimp (peeled and deveined), thawed if needed
* 12 oz cream cheese
* 3/4 cup heavy cream
* 1 cup tomato sauce
* 2 chipotle chiles in adobo, plus 1 tbsp adobo sauce
* 3/4 cup water
* 2 tbsp lime juice

## Steps

* If serving with rice, start White Basmati Rice now so it is ready when the shrimp is done.
* Add the 2 1/4 lb shrimp to a large bowl with enough warm water to cover and 2 tbsp lime juice. Soak for 15 minutes (use warm, not hot, water if the shrimp is still partially frozen).
* When about 5 minutes remain on the shrimp soak, make the sauce: add 12 oz cream cheese, 3/4 cup heavy cream, 1 cup tomato sauce, 2 chipotle chiles, 1 tbsp adobo sauce, and 3/4 cup water to a blender. Blend until completely smooth.
* Drain the shrimp well, return it to the bowl, and pat dry with paper towels so it sears instead of steaming.
* Heat a large skillet over medium-high heat and add a light drizzle of oil. Add all the shrimp in an even layer, then season with garlic powder, salt, and black pepper to taste.
* Cook the shrimp for about 2 minutes, stirring frequently, until all pieces are pink and opaque.
* Increase the heat to high, pour in all of the blended chipotle sauce, and stir to coat the shrimp evenly.
* Reduce heat to medium and simmer for 5 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the sauce is hot, smooth, and lightly thickened.
* Taste the sauce and adjust with an extra pinch of salt or black pepper if needed, then serve hot.
