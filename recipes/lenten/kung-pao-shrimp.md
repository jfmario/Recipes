# Kung Pao Shrimp

This lenten take on Kung Pao swaps chicken for shrimp. Frozen cooked, peeled, and deveined shrimp is convenient, but it must be fully thawed, well drained, and patted dry or the wok will steam instead of sear. Because the shrimp is already cooked, it goes in after the sauce has thickened so it only needs a short toss to heat through and pick up glaze — longer cooking makes it tough.

At this batch size, work in two rounds so the pan stays hot and the shrimp heat evenly.

## Ingredients

* 2 lb (907 g) frozen cooked peeled and deveined shrimp (thawed)
* 2 tbsp + 2 tbsp peanut oil (or other neutral high-heat oil), split for two batches
* 4 garlic cloves, minced
* 2 tsp fresh ginger, finely chopped
* 12 to 16 dried red chilies, cut into short pieces with most seeds discarded
* 6 green onions, cut into 3/4-inch pieces, whites and greens separated
* 1 tbsp ground Sichuan peppercorns, plus more to taste
* 1 1/2 cups roasted unsalted peanuts
* 1 tbsp + 1 tsp cornstarch
* 2 tbsp light soy sauce
* 3 tbsp dark soy sauce
* 1/4 cup Chinese black vinegar
* 2 tbsp Shaoxing wine
* 6 tbsp sugar
* 1 tsp sesame oil

## Steps

* If serving with rice, start it first so it is ready when the stir fry finishes.
* Thaw the 2 lb (907 g) frozen cooked shrimp safely: either overnight in the refrigerator in a colander set over a bowl (best), or in a sealed bag submerged in cold water, changing the water every 30 minutes until thawed. Drain well, then spread the shrimp on a towel-lined tray and pat very dry. If any shrimp are extra large, halve them crosswise so they match bite-size pieces.
* In a bowl, whisk 1 tbsp + 1 tsp cornstarch with 2 tbsp light soy sauce until smooth, then whisk in 3 tbsp dark soy sauce, 1/4 cup Chinese black vinegar, 2 tbsp Shaoxing wine, 6 tbsp sugar, and 1 tsp sesame oil. Whisk in 2/3 cup water and set the bowl beside the stove.
* Heat a wok or large skillet over high heat until very hot. Add 2 tbsp peanut oil, then add 2 minced garlic cloves, 1 tsp chopped ginger, and 6 to 8 dried chilies. Stir-fry for 30 seconds until fragrant. Add the white parts from 3 green onions and toss for 15 seconds.
* Re-whisk the sauce, pour in half of it, and add 1 1/2 tsp ground Sichuan peppercorns. Stir constantly over high heat for 1 1/2 to 2 1/2 minutes, until the sauce is a thick, glossy syrup.
* Add half of the dried shrimp. Toss and stir-fry constantly for 45 to 60 seconds, just until the shrimp are hot through and evenly coated; do not keep cooking once they are hot.
* Add 3/4 cup peanuts and the green parts from 3 green onions. Toss for 20 to 30 seconds, transfer the first batch to a platter, and keep warm.
* Cook the second batch the same way: heat the remaining 2 tbsp peanut oil, then stir-fry the remaining 2 minced garlic cloves, 1 tsp ginger, and 6 to 8 dried chilies for 30 seconds. Add the white parts from the remaining 3 green onions and toss for 15 seconds.
* Re-whisk the sauce, pour in the remainder, and add the remaining 1 1/2 tsp ground Sichuan peppercorns. Stir until syrupy as before. Add the remaining shrimp and toss 45 to 60 seconds until hot through and glazed.
* Finish with the remaining 3/4 cup peanuts and the green parts from the remaining 3 green onions. Toss 20 to 30 seconds, then combine both batches on the platter. Taste and add an extra pinch of ground Sichuan peppercorns if you want more numbing heat. Serve immediately.
