# Seven Spice

This blend leans sweet-aromatic from cinnamon, allspice, and cloves, with earthy coriander and cumin and a little bite from black pepper. Use it anywhere you would reach for a Lebanese-style baharat or “seven spice” rub: kofta, chicken, lamb, chickpea stews, or a teaspoon stirred into rice before cooking.

For the clearest flavor, use spices that still smell strong when you open the jar. If anything reads flat or dusty, replace it before you mix the batch.

## Ingredients

* 2 tbsp ground allspice
* 2 tbsp ground coriander
* 2 tbsp ground cinnamon
* 3 tsp ground black pepper
* 3 tsp ground cloves
* 3 tsp ground cumin
* 3 tsp ground nutmeg

## Steps

* Set out a small bowl, a whisk or fork, and an airtight jar large enough for about 10 tbsp (5/8 cup) of finished blend.
* Add 2 tbsp ground allspice, 2 tbsp ground coriander, 2 tbsp ground cinnamon, 3 tsp ground black pepper, 3 tsp ground cloves, 3 tsp ground cumin, and 3 tsp ground nutmeg to the bowl.
* Whisk thoroughly for 1 to 2 minutes, breaking up any clumps (nutmeg in particular), until the color looks even and you do not see streaks of a single spice.
* Transfer the mixture to the jar, seal, label with the date, and store in a cool, dark place. Use within about 6 months for best potency; after that it is still safe but will taste milder.
